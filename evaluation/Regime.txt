1) What is “Market Regime”

A market regime is the dominant macro environment that drives asset returns.

It compresses complex inputs (growth, inflation, rates, geopolitics) into a single actionable state.

Common regimes
Early-cycle → growth recovering, rates low
Mid-cycle → stable growth, moderate inflation
Late-cycle → strong growth, rising rates
Recession → growth falling, easing policy
Stagflation → weak growth + high inflation
In your dataset (example)
Strong labour + rising oil + geopolitics
→ Late-cycle / higher-for-longer / elevated risk
2) Expected Outcome (Why it matters)

Using regimes gives you:

a) Consistent strategy mapping
Regime → sectors → assets
(no ad-hoc decisions)
b) Better signal-to-noise
Filters noisy news into clear positioning
c) Portfolio alignment
Avoids contradictions (e.g. growth stocks + rising rates)
d) Systematic output

Final output becomes structured:

{
  "regime": "...",
  "strategy": "...",
  "sectors": {...},
  "portfolio": [...]
}

how often the regime should be checked, or what's the trigger to re-check
1) How often should you check the regime?

Use a hybrid approach (time-based + event-driven). Pure schedules are too slow; pure triggers are too noisy.

Baseline cadence
Daily (light check)
Recompute signals, not full portfolio
Weekly (full regime review)
Reclassify regime
Re-evaluate sector tilts
Monthly (portfolio rebalance)
Adjust weights / positions

This mirrors how institutional PMs operate:
signal monitoring ≠ portfolio turnover.

2) Event-Driven Triggers (More Important)

You should override the schedule when key variables shift.

A. Macro data shocks

Trigger if:

Jobs / inflation materially surprise
Rate expectations shift

Example:

Strong payrolls → rates ↑ → regime shift toward late-cycle
B. Geopolitical escalation

From your dataset:

Iran conflict / oil supply risk

Trigger if:

Oil spike > ~5–10%
ռազմական escalation headlines cluster

Impact:

Forces immediate regime reassessment
C. Market price signals (fastest indicator)

Trigger thresholds:

Bond yields move > 20–30bps in short time
Equity index drawdown > 5%
VIX spike > 25–30

These often lead macro data

D. Internal signal drift (your system)

Since you already have:

macro_signals
market intelligence

Add:

{
  "regime_score_change": 0.25
}

Trigger re-check if:

Composite score changes > 20–30%
